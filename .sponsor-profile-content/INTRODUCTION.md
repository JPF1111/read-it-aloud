## The flagship: Read It Aloud

**[Read It Aloud](https://github.com/JPF1111/read-it-aloud)** is a free, privacy-first browser extension that turns any text on a webpage into clean, natural-sounding audio.

I built it because I have ADHD.

Reading long articles, court filings, and research papers on a screen was a constant uphill battle — the words would blur, my focus would drift, and 20 minutes would vanish on 3 paragraphs of progress. Audio cut through the wall.

The extension is now serving people with dyslexia, ADHD, post-concussion sensitivity, visual fatigue, language learners, and anyone who'd rather listen than read.

It's **free and always will be**. No ads. No analytics. No data collection — the architecture *cannot* collect data, by design. That's a structural privacy commitment, not a marketing claim.

Chrome is shipping to the Web Store now. Edge, Brave, Opera, Firefox, and Safari (macOS + iOS) are next.

## Why this needs sponsorship

I could have built Read It Aloud as a $5/month SaaS. I didn't. I could have taken VC. I didn't. I could run analytics, sell anonymized data, A/B test dark patterns to lift retention. I refuse to.

Each one of those refusals costs revenue. They're deliberate — built so the tool stays free and private for the people who need it most, who often can't afford another subscription on top of medication, therapy, school costs, or rent.

That model only works if the underlying work gets supported some other way.

**That's what sponsorship is for.** Independence costs money. Sponsorship is the structure that funds the choice to stay independent.
